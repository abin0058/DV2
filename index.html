Australia’s Housing Affordability and Living Pressure

Australia is currently facing growing housing affordability and living cost pressures, largely driven by rising house prices, increasing rental costs, population growth, migration, and inflation that have outpaced wage growth in many regions. This visualisation project examines the relationship between these factors across Australia by analysing median house prices, rental affordability, wages, homelessness, migration, and population growth to highlight regional differences in housing stress and economic pressure. Using a choropleth map to compare median house prices by state, a proportional symbol map to display rental affordability by city, and a dot map to illustrate migration and population growth concentration, this project provides a spatial overview of the housing challenges affecting Australian residents and offers insights into broader socioeconomic trends influencing affordability nationwide.
